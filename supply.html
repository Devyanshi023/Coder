Supply chain cyber attacks

Cybersecurity has become a major concern for organizations across the world, and supply chain cyber attacks pose a significant threat to businesses.


Supply chains are complex systems with multiple actors and touchpoints, making them vulnerable to cyber attacks.


A supply chain cyber attack occurs when a hacker gains unauthorized access to a company's supply chain network to steal sensitive data, disrupt operations, or introduce malware.


This has dramatically changed the attack surface of the typical enterprise in the past few years, with more suppliers and service providers touching sensitive data than ever before.


Attackers now have more resources and tools at their disposal than ever before, creating a perfect storm.



  • SQL Injection Attacks
  • Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) Attacks
  • Zero-Day Exploits
  • Watering Hole Attacks
  • Cryptojacking Attacks
  • Third-Party Attacks
  • Insider Attacks
  • Social Engineering Attacks
  • Buffer Overflow


Wanna Know About The Current Stats of These Attacks?

66% of the reported incidents involved attacks on suppliers’ code.

58% of supply chain incidents target customer data – including personal data and intellectual property.

62% of attacks exploit the trust of customers in their suppliers.

Let's talk about Protection!

WORKING OF SUPPLY CHAIN ATTACKS



Easy-peasy Protection is in your Hands!!

Here are 10 simple tips for identifying and preventing these scams.



1. Conduct a Risk Assessment

Identify the weak points in your supply chain network and assess the risks associated with those points.


2. Regularly update software and firmware

Once a cybersecurity plan is in place, you should implement policies to update software and firmware regularly. This will help ensure that all devices and systems are up to date with the latest security patches.


3. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds another level of security by requiring two or more authentication methods, such as a password and a fingerprint.


4. Encrypt data

Data encryption can help protect sensitive information from being intercepted or stolen. Use encryption techniques to protect data at rest and in transit, such as SSL or TLS.


5. Conduct Due Diligence

Conduct due diligence on all vendors to ensure they have strong cyber security protocols in place.


6. Use Secure Communication Channels

Use secure communication channels to transfer sensitive data between parties.


7. Install firewalls

Firewalls are an effective way to prevent external attacks, acting as a shield between your computer and an attacker. Both desktop firewalls and network firewalls, when used together, can bolster your security and reduce the chances of a hacker infiltrating your environment.


8. Build in redundancy

To protect against system failures or attacks, build in redundancy by using backup systems or multiple devices. This can help ensure that essential functions continue to operate even if one system or device fails.


9. Implement access controls

Another way to protect your devices is to implement access controls. Use policy-based controls and role-based access to restrict access to sensitive data and systems.


10. Implement Incident Response Plans

Have a plan in place to respond to a cyber attack to minimize damage.
