IoT based cyber attacks

IoT (Internet of Things) based cyber attacks are those attacks that occur when devices with internet connectivity are hacked or exploited by attackers to access sensitive information or cause harm.


It is a threat to systems that make use of such devices, including smart homes, hospitals, automobiles, and other related systems.


They can impact privacy, safety, and data security.


These pose a serious threat to organizations and individuals worldwide and can cause significant financial and reputational damage.


As organizations in virtually every industry increase their operational reliance on IoT devices, security teams must account for both simple and complex risk factors specific to the world of IoT



  • Botnets
  • Convergence
  • Eavesdropping
  • Remote code execution
  • Brute-force attacks
  • Backdoor access
  • Physical access
  • SQL Injection
  • Buffer Overflow


Wanna Know About The Current Stats of These Attacks?

The first two months of 2023 have seen a 41% increase in the average number of weekly attacks per organization targeting IoT devices, compared to 2022.

On average, every week 54% of organizations suffer from attempted cyber attacks targeting IoT devices

IoT devices in European organizations are the most targeted, followed by those in APAC and Latin America-based organizations.

WORKING OF IoT BASED ATTACKS



Easy-peasy Protection is in your Hands!!

Here are 10 simple tips for identifying and preventing these scams.



1.Develop a cybersecurity plan

The first step in protecting your devices from IoT-based cyber attacks is to create a cybersecurity plan. The plan should include all devices and systems, as well as the type of data they process or store.


2. Regularly update software and firmware

Once a cybersecurity plan is in place, you should implement policies to update software and firmware regularly. This will help ensure that all devices and systems are up to date with the latest security patches.


3. Implement strong passwords

One of the most commonly exploited vulnerabilities in IoT systems is weak passwords. To protect your devices, implement a policy requiring strong passwords for all users.


4. Encrypt data

Data encryption can help protect sensitive information from being intercepted or stolen. Use encryption techniques to protect data at rest and in transit, such as SSL or TLS.


5. Monitor network traffic

Regularly monitoring network traffic can help you detect and prevent unauthorized access. Use network monitoring tools to keep track of all incoming and outgoing data.


6. Conduct regular vulnerability assessments

Regular vulnerability assessments can help you identify any weaknesses or vulnerabilities in your systems and devices. Use a variety of tools to conduct regular assessments and identify potential threats.


7. Install firewalls

Firewalls are an effective way to prevent external attacks, acting as a shield between your computer and an attacker. Both desktop firewalls and network firewalls, when used together, can bolster your security and reduce the chances of a hacker infiltrating your environment.


8. Build in redundancy

To protect against system failures or attacks, build in redundancy by using backup systems or multiple devices. This can help ensure that essential functions continue to operate even if one system or device fails.


9. Implement access controls

Another way to protect your devices is to implement access controls. Use policy-based controls and role-based access to restrict access to sensitive data and systems.


10. Use security technologies

Use security technologies such as intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDS/IPS) and antivirus software to help protect your devices and systems.
