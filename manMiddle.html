Man-in-the-Middle cyber attacks

Man-in-the-middle (MITM) is a type of cyber attack in which an attacker intercepts communication between two parties, with the intention of intercepting, altering, or redirecting the traffic without either party's knowledge.


This attack allows the attacker to eavesdrop, steal information, or impersonate one of the communicating parties.


MITM attacks are serious and can result in identity theft, financial loss, and legal ramifications.


The goal of an attack is to steal personal information, such as login credentials, account details and credit card numbers.


Information obtained during an attack could be used for many purposes, including identity theft, unapproved fund transfers or an illicit password change.

WORKING OF MAN-IN-THE-MIDDLE ATTACKS



Easy-peasy Protection is in your Hands!!

Here are 10 simple tips for identifying and preventing these scams.



1. Use end-to-end encryption

Using end-to-end encryption technologies, such as TLS/SSL, that use digital certificates can encrypt all communications between two parties.


2. Regularly update software and firmware

Once a cybersecurity plan is in place, you should implement policies to update software and firmware regularly. This will help ensure that all devices and systems are up to date with the latest security patches.


3. Use VPN

A virtual private network (VPN) encrypts traffic between two devices over the internet and creates a secure connection.


4. HTTPS connections

Use websites with HTTPS security protocol.


5. Monitor network traffic

Regularly monitoring network traffic can help you detect and prevent unauthorized access. Use network monitoring tools to keep track of all incoming and outgoing data.


6. Conduct regular vulnerability assessments

Regular vulnerability assessments can help you identify any weaknesses or vulnerabilities in your systems and devices. Use a variety of tools to conduct regular assessments and identify potential threats.


7. Be wary of public Wi-Fi

Be cautious about using public Wi-Fi networks, as they are easy targets for MITM attacks.


8. Avoid unsecured sites

Avoid visiting unsecured websites that are risky and vulnerable to MITM attacks.


9. Avoid clicking on links from unexpected sources

Be cautious when clicking on links or downloading attachments from unexpected sources, as they could be malicious.


10. Check for SSL cert authenticity

Check whether the SSL certificate presented by a website is authentic before sharing any sensitive information.
